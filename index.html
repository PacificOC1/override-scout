Autonomous points calculation

Master equation — expected auto points per alliance

RawPts = (Robot1_avgPins + Robot2_avgPins) × 5 + Robot1_avgBonuses + Robot2_avgBonuses ConsFactor = (Robot1_cons + Robot2_cons) / 10 → range 0.2–1.0 ExpectedPts = RawPts × ConsFactor AutoBonus = 12 pts awarded to higher-scoring alliance (SC7) → factored in as: if ExpectedPts_Red > ExpectedPts_Blue, Red +12

Each placed alliance pin = 5 pts (SC3). Bonuses are added directly — they are whole point values from game manual scoring. ConsFactor scales raw output down for erratic robots; two robots at 5/5 = ConsFactor 1.0 (no reduction).

Consistency factor

ConsFactor = (R1_cons + R2_cons) / 10 Examples: Both at 5/5 → (5+5)/10 = 1.00 (full output, no reduction) Both at 3/5 → (3+3)/10 = 0.60 (40% haircut on raw pts) Both at 1/5 → (1+1)/10 = 0.20 (80% haircut — very erratic)

A team averaging 8 pins with consistency 2/5 is riskier than one averaging 6 pins at 5/5. The factor compresses expected output to reflect real-match variance. Enter consistency from your scouting observations.

Autonomous bonus inclusion

FinalPts_Red = ExpectedPts_Red + (ExpectedPts_Red > ExpectedPts_Blue ? 12 : 0) FinalPts_Blue = ExpectedPts_Blue + (ExpectedPts_Blue > ExpectedPts_Red ? 12 : 0) Tie → each gets +6 Points win probability = FinalPts_Red / (FinalPts_Red + FinalPts_Blue)

AWP probability calculation (independent from points)

AWP_alliance% = (R1_AWP% / 100) × (R2_AWP% / 100) × consistencyWeight × 100 consistencyWeight = (R1_cons + R2_cons) / 10 (same factor as points) Interpretation: R1 = 80%, R2 = 70%, both cons 4/5: → (0.80 × 0.70) × (4+4)/10 × 100 → 0.56 × 0.80 × 100 = 44.8%

AWP requires BOTH robots to succeed (SC8: 7+ pins, 3+ goals with 2+ each, neither touching perimeter). The joint probability multiplies individual rates — if either robot fails, the alliance loses AWP. Consistency further discounts the probability for unreliable partners. AWP earns WP (ranking points), NOT match score — displayed separately for this reason.

Worked example

Red: Robot A → 4 avg pins, 2 bonuses, 80% AWP, cons 4/5 Robot B → 3 avg pins, 0 bonuses, 60% AWP, cons 3/5 RawPts = (4+3)×5 + (2+0) = 37 ConsFactor = (4+3)/10 = 0.70 ExpectedPts = 37 × 0.70 = 25.9 AWP% = (0.80 × 0.60) × 0.70 × 100 = 33.6% Blue: Robot C → 5 avg pins, 1 bonus, 40% AWP, cons 5/5 Robot D → 5 avg pins, 1 bonus, 70% AWP, cons 4/5 RawPts = (5+5)×5 + (1+1) = 52 ConsFactor = (5+4)/10 = 0.90 ExpectedPts = 52 × 0.90 = 46.8 AWP% = (0.40 × 0.70) × 0.90 × 100 = 25.2% Auto bonus → Blue wins points (46.8 > 25.9) → Blue +12 FinalPts: Red 25.9, Blue 58.8 Points win%: Red 31%, Blue 69% AWP: Red 33.6%, Blue 25.2% (Red more likely to get AWP despite losing points)

Notice: Red has a higher AWP probability than Blue even though Blue dominates the point battle. This is realistic — a disciplined but lower-scoring alliance can still consistently hit AWP criteria, earning the ranking point even in a match they lose.

What the model does not capture

• Toggle control — 10 pts per owned yellow pin, massive swing, unpredictable in autonomous
• Counter-routing — opponent's psi can physically block your long goal approach
• Partner goal conflicts — two robots targeting the same goal simultaneously
• Sig-specific AWP criteria — harder thresholds release September 3 2026
• Match-specific variance — 15 seconds leaves no error recovery time

Recalibrate as you collect data: if the model consistently over- or under-predicts, adjust the pin coefficient (currently 5, matches game manual) or the consistency divisor.
